About us

Steps of Joy Pediatric Physical Therapy is led by Ronit Shkedi, PT, who brings more than 20 years of experience helping children and families reach their movement goals.

We focus on professional evaluations and treatment, family guidance, realistic home programs, and care that fits your child’s personality, so therapy feels motivating, not intimidating. Whether you’re navigating early motor milestones, torticollis, developmental delays, or any questions, we’re here to partner with you.

Serving families in Bergen County, New Jersey and nearby communities.

Services

Care is individualized to your child’s age, goals, and daily routines, and explained in plain language you can use between sessions.

  • Gross motor & mobility

    All 1st year milestones, as well as walking, jumping, and playing age-appropriate games will be addressed, with attention to alignment, strength, and mobility.

  • Developmental support

    Help when milestones feel delayed or uneven, providing home exercises and guidance you can practice with your child every day.

  • Torticollis & plagiocephaly

    Parent education, gentle positioning, stretching, and strengthening are key to treating torticollis, which can range from mild to severe. We will help your child achieve symmetry in the head/neck and in play.

  • Balance & coordination

    Games and drills that build body awareness, stability, and confidence on the move.

  • Mommy and Me classes

    Small-group sessions where you and your baby or toddler explore movement, songs, and play together while building strength, early motor skills, and connection in a fun, supportive setting.

  • Virtual & home visits

    Virtual sessions bring coaching, demonstrations, and follow-up to you through secure video. In-home visits let us work in your child’s everyday environment when that best supports progress. We’ll help you choose what fits your schedule and goals.

Our approach

Play, patience, and evidence-based care, with goals that matter to your child and your family.

  • One-on-one attention and parent-friendly explanations
  • Techniques adapted to your child’s age and temperament
  • Collaboration with pediatricians and specialists when needed
  • Celebrating progress of all sizes along the way

FAQ

Quick answers. Reach out anytime for specifics about your child.

What happens at the first visit?

We review your concerns and your child’s history, observe movement and play, and outline a practical plan. You’ll leave with a clearer picture of goals and next steps.

Do parents stay for therapy?

Yes. Caregiver involvement is welcome, and we’ll show you how to support skills at home in ways that fit your routine.

How do I get started?

Call or email and we’ll discuss scheduling, paperwork, and any concerns you may have.
